Key Facts

ASCII uses 7 bits to represent 128 characters (0–127), including letters, digits, and symbols.
Unicode uses 16+ bits and can represent over 143,000 characters from all writing systems.
The first 128 Unicode characters are identical to ASCII.
Exam tip: In exams, you may be asked to convert between a character and its ASCII binary code. Remember: A=65, a=97, 0=48.
