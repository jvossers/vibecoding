Practice decomposition, abstraction, and algorithmic thinking.

Key Facts

Decomposition means breaking a complex problem down into smaller, more manageable sub-problems that can be solved individually.
Abstraction means removing unnecessary detail to focus on the important aspects. A London Tube map is a classic example — it removes geographic accuracy to show connections clearly.
Algorithmic thinking means creating a step-by-step solution (algorithm) to solve a problem. Each step must be clear, unambiguous, and in the correct order.
Exam tip: When asked to decompose a problem, list 3–4 specific sub-problems. When asked about abstraction, state what details you would remove AND why they're unnecessary.
