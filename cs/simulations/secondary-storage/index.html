Key Facts

Magnetic (HDD) stores data on spinning platters using magnetism. High capacity, low cost, but has moving parts.
Solid-state (SSD/USB) uses flash memory with no moving parts. Faster, more durable, but more expensive per GB.
Optical (CD/DVD/Blu-ray) uses a laser to read pits and lands on a disc. Good for distribution, limited capacity.
Exam tip: In comparison questions, consider: capacity, speed, cost, durability, and portability. Always relate your answer to the scenario given.
