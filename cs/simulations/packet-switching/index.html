Key Facts

Packet switching splits data into small packets. Each packet has a header containing the source, destination, and sequence number.
Packets can take different routes through the network and may arrive out of order. The receiving device uses sequence numbers to reassemble them.
Routers direct each packet towards its destination based on the destination address in the header.
Exam tip: You must describe how data is split into packets, how packets are routed, and how they are reassembled at the destination using sequence numbers.
